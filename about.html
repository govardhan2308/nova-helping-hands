Our Story

Over a Decade of Compassion

Founded in 2018, Nova Helping Hands began as a small response group to support regional schools affected by economic challenges.

A group of young graduates saw the widening gap in educational access and began crowdfunding to purchase basic books and bags. Witnessing the direct positive impact, we realized that communities required a more organized structure to support public health campaigns and environment initiatives.

Today, Nova Helping Hands has grown into a structured non-governmental organization operating across 12 cities. We partner with local governments, school districts, and global donors to provide holistic community care.

Our Vision

To establish a resilient society where every child gets access to education and communities thrive under healthy environmental parameters.

Our Mission

Delivering target-specific aids in basic nutrition, sound primary healthcare, environmental responsibility, and inclusive classroom education.

Dr. Elena Rostova

Founder & Executive Director

"True community development happens when we don't just hand out support, but build local capacities to sustain that support. Helping one child educates a generation."

Dr. Rostova holds a Ph.D. in Social Policy and has spent over fifteen years implementing rural outreach plans. Her vision of structural equality and environmental co-existence serves as the guiding light for Nova Helping Hands. She frequently collaborates with policy makers to address child nutrition shortages.

Our Journey

Key Milestones & Timeline

2018

Foundation & First School Project

Nova Helping Hands starts operations with 15 volunteers. Sourced text-books and stationery kits for 200 kids in Greenville rural sector.

2020

Clean Water & Sanitization Launch

Expanded support to water filtration. Built 8 bore-wells in high-fluoride affected villages, providing clean drinking water to 5,000 villagers.

2022

Health Clinics & Medical Outreaches

Partnered with private clinics to launch free health check-up buses. Reached over 10,000 patients and provided critical medicine supplies.

2025

National NGO Accreditation

Received 5-star ratings for financial transparency. Added tree plantation and municipal waste recycling programs into our main strategy.
